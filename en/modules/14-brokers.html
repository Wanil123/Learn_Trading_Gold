MODULE 14

Choosing your broker — without getting scammed

80% of offshore brokers are traps. Here's how to spot the good ones.

⚠️ The 3 golden rules
  1. Tier 1 regulation mandatory — FCA (UK), ASIC (Australia), CySEC (Cyprus-EU), BaFin (Germany), AMF (France)
  2. Segregation of client funds — your deposits are separated from the broker's operational accounts
  3. Guarantee fund — in Europe, protection up to 20,000€ (FSCS UK, FGDR France)

🏛️ Regulation: Tier 1 vs Tier 2 vs offshore

Tier 1 (safe)

  • FCA (UK) — the strictest in the world
  • ASIC (Australia) — strict, client protection
  • CySEC (Cyprus, EU) — ESMA rules: max leverage 1:20 on Gold, negative balance protection
  • BaFin (Germany), AMF (France) — very strict
  • NFA/CFTC (USA) — very strict, few brokers accept retail

Tier 2 (acceptable)

  • FMA (New Zealand), FSCA (South Africa), FINMA (Switzerland), DFSA (Dubai)

Tier 3 / offshore (AVOID)

  • SVG (Saint Vincent), Vanuatu, Seychelles, Belize, Comoros, Marshall Islands
  • No protection in case of bankruptcy
  • No leverage limits (1:500, 1:1000)
  • Legal recourse virtually impossible
The classic trap

A broker regulated by FCA but also opening an offshore entity (SVG, for example) for non-EU clients. Example: IC Markets Global (SVG) vs IC Markets EU (CySEC). ALWAYS check under which regulation YOU are opening your account, not just the broker's logo.

🚩 Red flags: run immediately

  • Leverage 1:500 or 1:1000 "with no restrictions"
  • Deposit bonus "100% up to $5000" — classic scam
  • Account managers who call you to encourage trading
  • "Free signals" provided by the broker
  • Weird proprietary platforms (not MT4/MT5/cTrader)
  • No mention of regulation on the website
  • Trustpilot reviews too perfect (bought reviews)
  • Withdrawals blocked with repeated "KYC verification"
  • "Prop firms" selling challenges at $500 and taking 80% of your profits — these are NOT brokers, it's a business model

📋 Process to choose YOUR broker

  1. Verify the broker is Tier 1 or 2 regulated
  2. Check the regulator's register directly (e.g.: register.fca.org.uk)
  3. Read withdrawal conditions and hidden fees
  4. Test the platform in demo for 2 weeks
  5. Make a small deposit ($100-200) and test a withdrawal before depositing more
  6. Check reviews on ForexFactory, Trustpilot, Reddit r/Forex (not just the broker's website)
  7. If everything is OK after 1 month → deposit your final capital

⚖️ Prop firms — important clarification

What prop firms are (FTMO, MyForexFunds, The5ers, FundedNext…)

These are NOT brokers. You pay for a challenge ($50-1000), you must pass 2 trading phases on a demo account, and if you succeed you receive a "funded" account (in reality often still demo, with partial payouts).

Pros: allows trading larger capital if you're already profitable
Cons: ~90% of challengers fail, rules are strict (max daily DD, time limit), many prop firms are themselves scams (MyForexFunds was shut down by the CFTC in 2023).

Rule: if you're not already profitable on a live account with your own money, a prop firm won't make you profitable. It's an accelerator for confirmed traders, not a training program.

🎯 Final recommendation

For your phase 4 ($500 live account), I recommend:

  • A CySEC or FCA regulated broker
  • ECN account if possible (tight spreads + commission)
  • Minimum deposit $100-500
  • MT4 or MT5 (you need it for the journal and Strategy Tester)

The 3 I suggest you shortlist: IC Markets, Pepperstone, Tickmill. Compare Gold spreads in real time before choosing.
