MODULE 02

Markets & Gold (XAUUSD)

Understand what you're trading before you trade it. Market structure, sessions, drivers, correlations.

What is Gold, exactly?

When you trade "Gold" through your CFD broker, you never touch any bars. You're trading a contract for difference that replicates the price of the London physical market (the LBMA Gold). The quoted price is for one troy ounce (31.1035 grams) denominated in US dollars — hence the ticker XAUUSD, where "XAU" is the ISO 4217 code for gold.

There are three main ways to get exposure to gold on the markets:

  • Gold Spot (XAUUSD) — the OTC interbank market, traded 23h/24 from Sunday evening to Friday evening. This is what your CFD broker replicates. No physical delivery, cash settlement, massive liquidity.
  • CME Futures (GC, MGC) — standardized contracts on the Chicago Mercantile Exchange. The GC (Gold Futures) is 100 ounces, the MGC (Micro Gold) is 10 ounces. Quarterly expirations, real visible volume, the price reference for many professionals.
  • ETFs (GLD, IAU) — for long-term investing, not intraday trading. Outside our scope here.

Contract size: what actually moves your account

On XAUUSD CFD, the standard convention is: 1 standard lot = 100 ounces. So if the gold price moves by 1 USD (e.g., from 2050 to 2051), one lot makes you gain or lose exactly 100 USD. That's the baseline rule to engrave in your brain.

As a corollary:

  • 0.10 lot (mini) → 1 USD of movement = 10 USD P&L
  • 0.01 lot (micro) → 1 USD of movement = 1 USD P&L
The "pip" trap on Gold

Unlike forex pairs, there is no universal definition of a pip on Gold. Some brokers call a "pip" a movement of 0.01 USD, others 0.10 USD, others 1 USD. Stop thinking in pips on XAUUSD. Think directly in USD of movement. If you want a stop 3 dollars from your entry, say "stop at 3 dollars," not "stop at 30 pips" or "300 pips" — you'll end up being off by a factor of 10.

Spreads and reputable brokers

The spread on XAUUSD varies enormously depending on the broker, the session, and volatility. During quiet hours with a serious ECN, you can see 15 to 25 cents of spread (0.15 to 0.25 USD). On news events, it can spike to 1-2 USD or more. With an aggressive market maker, you'll pay double at all times.

Among brokers reputed for serious Gold trading (solid regulation, decent execution, reasonable spreads): IC Markets, Pepperstone, Tickmill, Swissquote, Saxo Bank. These are not recommendations: always do your own regulatory checks (FCA, ASIC, FINMA, CySEC Tier 1).

Leverage: your worst friend

Since the 2018 ESMA reform, the maximum leverage allowed for a European retail trader on gold is 1:20. With offshore brokers, you can find 1:500 or 1:1000. That's not a gift — it's a permit for financial suicide.

The survival rule: your effective leverage (total exposure / capital) should never exceed 1:10, and ideally stay around 1:3 to 1:5. Leverage doesn't increase your edge — it just amplifies the magnitude, gains and losses alike. If your strategy is losing, leverage sends you to the graveyard faster. If it's winning, it stays winning without excessive leverage.

On top of this, there are the two LBMA Gold Fixings, when the London reference price is set: 10:30 AM and 3:00 PM London time. A lot of institutional liquidity flows at these times — sometimes you see brutal moves in a matter of minutes.

The real fundamental drivers of Gold

Forget the far-fetched theories. Here's what actually moves the price of gold, in order of importance:

  1. US real rates (driver #1) — measured by 10-year TIPS. Gold is a non-yielding asset; when real rates rise, holding gold becomes costly (opportunity cost), so the price drops. When they fall, gold rises. This is the most historically robust relationship.
  2. Inflation expectations — gold is perceived as an inflation hedge. But watch out: it's unexpected inflation that matters, and the relationship isn't linear.
  3. DXY (Dollar Index) — gold is priced in USD, so when the dollar weakens, gold becomes cheaper in other currencies → demand → price rises.
  4. Geopolitics — wars, crises, tensions. The "safe haven" effect is real but often short-term, unless it's a major crisis.
  5. Central banks — BRICS nations, China, Russia, India have been accumulating gold since 2022. Massive structural demand.
  6. Physical demand — jewelry (India, China), industry, ETFs. Evolves slowly but provides a floor.

Daily volatility: what to expect

The daily ATR (Average True Range) of XAUUSD typically sits between 150 and 300 "broker pips" (i.e., 15 to 30 USD of movement). On calm days, you might see a total range of 10 USD. On FOMC or CPI days, you can see 50 to 80 USD, sometimes more. Size your stops based on this reality: placing a stop 2 USD from the price on H1 is virtually guaranteed to get stopped out on noise.

High-impact news — protect yourself

Certain releases literally make Gold explode within seconds: FOMC (Fed decisions and minutes), CPI (US inflation), NFP (US employment, first Friday of the month), PCE (the Fed's preferred inflation indicator), Powell speeches. Simple rule: don't take any trade within the 30 minutes before or 15 minutes after these events, unless you have a strategy specifically designed for it. Spreads triple, slippage explodes, and your stop may not be honored.

Economic calendar

Use Forex Factory, Investing.com, or MyFXBook for the news calendar. Filter on USD + high impact. Check it every morning before you sit down at your screen. It takes 2 minutes and saves accounts.

What you should take away

Gold is a mature, dense market driven by US real rates and the dollar. It's primarily traded between 07:00 and 20:00 UTC, with a peak between 12:00 and 16:00. One standard lot = 100 ounces = 100 USD per dollar of movement. Leverage is your worst enemy if you don't respect it. And before every session, you check your economic calendar. Always.
