MODULE 08

Complete Gold Strategy

London/NY Liquidity Sweep + FVG Retest — end-to-end trade plan on XAUUSD

🎯 Objective of this module

You will learn a concrete, reproducible strategy with explicit rules applied to Gold (XAUUSD). This is not THE perfect strategy — it does not exist. It is a solid educational example that you must backtest, adjust, and make your own. The goal: to show you how to assemble the building blocks from previous modules into a coherent plan.

The strategy in one sentence

We wait for a liquidity sweep of a significant high/low during a Killzone (London or NY), we validate with a CHoCH/MSS on a lower timeframe, and we enter on the retest of the FVG created by the displacement, in the OTE zone.

1. Directional bias — Daily and H4

Before anything else: you must have a clear bias on the market direction. No bias, no trade.

  • D1: is the structure HH/HL (uptrend) or LH/LL (downtrend)? Note the major Liquidity zone (last D1 high/low).
  • H4: same analysis. This is your operational bias timeframe.
  • DXY H4: the Dollar Index. Gold and DXY are negatively correlated (~-0.7 to -0.85). If you are long Gold, you want a bearish DXY.
  • US10Y (US 10-year yield): also negatively correlated with Gold. Rising yields = Gold under pressure.

Absolute rule: you ONLY take trades in the direction of the H4 bias. No counter-trend.

2. Session preparation — before London Open (07:00 UTC)

  1. Mark the Asian session highs and lows (23:00–07:00 UTC) on your chart.
  2. Mark the unmitigated H4 and D1 highs/lows = major Liquidity zones.
  3. Check the economic calendar (ForexFactory). If FOMC, CPI, NFP, PPI or Powell speech within 2 hours → no trade.
  4. Check DXY: direction, structure, key levels.
  5. Define your scenario A (long) and scenario B (short) with precise zones.

3. Entry setup — M15 / M5 / M1

  1. Liquidity sweep: price pierces a previous high/low (Asian, H4, or equal highs/lows) with a rapid wick, then rejects.
  2. CHoCH/MSS: on M5 or M1, price must break the internal structure opposite to the sweep — with an impulsive displacement.
  3. FVG identified: the displacement leaves a visible Fair Value Gap.
  4. Entry: limit order on the retest of the FVG (or the OB that created the displacement), ideally in the OTE zone (61–79% Fibonacci of the impulsive leg).

4. Trade management

  • Stop Loss: just beyond the extreme swing of the sweep (below the sweep low for a long, above the high for a short) + a buffer of 20–30 pips to absorb the Gold spread and volatility.
  • TP1 at 1R: secure 33% of the position, move SL to Break Even.
  • TP2 at 2R: secure another 33%.
  • TP3: next Liquidity level (next H4 or D1 high/low).
  • Time stop: if after 2 hours the trade is not clearly moving in your favor, you close. A good setup moves fast.

5. Optimal trading windows

  • London Killzone: 07:00–10:00 UTC — the best window for Gold, high volume, directional moves.
  • NY Killzone: 13:30–16:00 UTC — second best, watch out for US news at 13:30 and 14:00.
  • Silver Bullet: 15:00–16:00 UTC — a closely watched window.
  • Avoid: 10:00–13:00 UTC (between London and NY, range), 20:00–23:00 UTC (late NY, slow), pure Asian session.

6. Money management — non-negotiable

⚠️ Strict rules — break them and you blow your account
  • 1% max risk per trade. Not 2%. Not "just this once."
  • 2 losing trades max in a day → you close the platform.
  • 3 trades max per day, no matter what.
  • Target Winrate: 45–55%. If someone sells you 80%, it is either a lie or overfitting.
  • Target Expectancy: +0.3R to +0.5R per trade on average.

8. Concrete example — long trade on Gold

📊 Detailed pedagogical scenario

Context: Monday 09:30 UTC. Gold in D1 uptrend (clear HH/HL). H4 also in uptrend, last HL at 1995.00, last HH at 2008.00. DXY in H4 downtrend. No news before 14:30.

Observation: during the Asian session, price forms a low at 2000.50 (obvious sell-side Liquidity). At 08:15 UTC, during the London Killzone, price plunges to 1999.20 — sweep of the Asian low — then violently rejects over 3 M5 candles. An FVG forms between 2001.00 and 2002.50 during the displacement. On M1, a bullish CHoCH validates at 2003.00.

Plan:

  • Limit entry at 2002.00 (middle of the FVG, OTE zone of the 1999.20 → 2003.50 leg)
  • Stop Loss at 1998.70 (below sweep low + 50-pip buffer)
  • SL distance = 330 pips ($3.30 on Gold)
  • TP1 at 2005.30 (1R)
  • TP2 at 2008.60 (2R, taking buy-side Liquidity at H4 high)
  • TP3 at 2012.00 (next D1 level)

Position sizing: capital $10,000, risk 1% = $100. SL distance = $3.30. On Gold, 1 standard lot = 100 oz, so $1 of movement = $100 P&L per lot. Size = $100 / (3.30 x 100) = 0.30 lot. If SL hit: loss of $99 ≈ 1% ✓.

Possible outcome: TP1 hit (+$33), SL moved to BE, TP2 hit (+$66), TP3 not reached (SL BE on the last tranche). Total gain ≈ +$99 i.e. +1R on the trade. Not spectacular — but reproducible, and that is the goal.

9. MANDATORY backtesting before going live

  • Minimum 100 manually backtested trades on historical Gold data (last 6 months).
  • 2 months on demo in real conditions (same hours, same strict rules).
  • If your Expectancy is negative after 100 trades → the strategy does not suit YOU. Adapt it or switch.
  • Mandatory trading journal: every entry, why you took it, the result, the emotion you felt.

10. Strategy limitations — be clear-eyed

🚫 What can kill this strategy
  • Low-volatility consolidation: no real impulse, sweeps become ambiguous.
  • Weekend gaps: can stop you out with no recourse.
  • Unexpected news (geopolitics, central banks): can invalidate any setup in seconds.
  • High Gold spread: 30–50 pips at many brokers. It literally eats into low-RR trades. Choose a broker with Gold spread < 25 pips.
  • Killzone slippage: executions can slip several pips during fast moves.
🥇 Conclusion — this strategy is an EXAMPLE

This strategy is a pedagogical example, not a revealed truth. The goal is not for you to copy it blindly, but to understand how to build YOUR own strategy with these building blocks: multi-TF bias, Liquidity, sweep, structural confirmation, precise entry, rigorous management. Backtest, adapt to your personality (scalper vs swing), find YOUR edge. And above all: never trade live what you have not proven in backtesting and demo.
