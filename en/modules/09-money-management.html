MODULE 09

Money Management — The Real Pillar

80% of your profitability comes from here. Not from your strategy. Not from your indicators. From here.

The truth nobody wants to hear

You can have the best strategy in the world: without money management, you will eventually blow up. And you can have a mediocre strategy: with rock-solid money management, you can survive long enough to become good. It is that unfair. Risk management is not "a module in the course" — it IS the course. Everything else is decoration.

1. The 1% rule — non-negotiable

The rule is simple: you never risk more than 1% of your capital on a single trade. Not 2%, not 3%, not "exceptionally 5% because the setup is incredible." 1%. Why this exact number? Because the math is brutal and does not negotiate.

With 1% risk per trade, it would take 100 consecutive losses to wipe your account. Statistically, if your strategy has even a tiny edge (say 45% Winrate with a 1:1.5 Risk/Reward), the probability of stringing 100 losses together is on the order of 10 to the power of -26. In other words: impossible. You are mathematically protected against ruin.

At 2%, it takes 50 consecutive losses. That is acceptable for a confirmed trader who knows their stats down to the decimal. At 3%, you enter the danger zone. Above 3%, ruin is no longer a possibility, it is a certainty on a bad streak. And bad streaks will come. Always.

Golden rule

Until you have 200 documented trades with a positive and stable Expectancy, you risk 0.5% per trade. Period. This is not timidity, it is survival.

2. The math of asymmetric recovery

Here is the table that should be tattooed on every trader's arm. When you lose X%, you need far more than X% to get back to breakeven. Because you are calculating the required gain on a reduced capital base.

Drawdown sufferedGain needed to recoverVerdict
-10%+11.1%Manageable
-25%+33.3%Hard but possible
-50%+100%Very difficult
-75%+300%Nearly impossible
-90%+900%Game over

Look carefully at -50%. If your account is at $10,000 and falls to $5,000, you do not need "another -50%" to get back. You need +100%. To double your capital. How many profitable traders make +100% per year? A handful. And you, having just lost half your account, are supposed to do better than them? No. That is why money management is not optional.

The asymmetry trap

Your brain thinks linearly: "-20% means +20% to recover." Wrong. It is +25%. This asymmetry kills more accounts than anything else. The deeper the Drawdown, the more the recovery becomes mathematically out of reach.

3. Risk/Reward — the other mathematical lever

Winrate is overrated. Risk/Reward is underrated. Look:

  • With a 1:2 Risk/Reward, a Winrate of 34% is enough to break even (before fees).
  • With a 1:3 Risk/Reward, 25% Winrate is enough.
  • With a 1:1 Risk/Reward, you need over 50% to be profitable (fees included).

You want a high Winrate for your ego? Take a 1:1 Risk/Reward. You want to be profitable? Take a minimum 1:2 Risk/Reward and accept losing more often than winning. It is counterintuitive, but this is how pros make money: lots of small losses, a few big wins. Never take a trade below 1:1.5 Risk/Reward. If your setup does not allow at least 1:1.5, it is not a setup.

4. Expectancy (Van Tharp) — the only number that matters

Expectancy is the average gain you can expect per trade. Formula:

E = (Winrate x Avg Win) - (Loss rate x Avg Loss)

Concrete example: 45% Winrate, avg win = 2R, avg loss = 1R.

E = (0.45 x 2) - (0.55 x 1) = 0.90 - 0.55 = +0.35R per trade

This means on average, each trade earns you 0.35 times your risk. If you risk $100 per trade, you earn on average $35 per trade. Over 200 trades, +$7,000 in expected value. If your E is negative, no position sizing, no magic will make you profitable. You are losing in slow motion. If E ≤ 0 → losing strategy, period.

The ultimate test

Before every trade, ask yourself: "If I took this setup 1,000 times, would the Expectancy be positive?" If you cannot answer yes with conviction, you are not trading an edge, you are gambling.

5. Position sizing — the universal formula

How many lots should you buy? This is not a matter of intuition, it is a formula:

Size = (Capital x Risk%) / (SL distance x Value per point)

For XAUUSD (1 standard lot = 100 ounces), the formula becomes:

Lot = Risk_USD / (SL_distance_USD x 100)

Concrete example: your account is at $10,000. You risk 1% = $100. Your entry is at 2,050, your SL at 2,040 (distance = $10).

Lot = 100 / (10 x 100) = 0.1 lot

You buy 0.1 lot. If SL is hit, you lose exactly $100. Not $150, not $80. $100. You never trade without doing this calculation. Ever. Use the built-in calculator to avoid mistakes — a decimal error on XAUUSD means 10x your intended risk.

6. Kelly Criterion — powerful and dangerous

The Kelly Criterion gives the optimal fraction of capital to risk in order to maximize long-term growth:

Kelly% = W - [(1-W) / R] where W = Winrate, R = win/loss ratio

Example: 50% Winrate, 2 Risk/Reward → Kelly = 0.5 - (0.5/2) = 0.25 = 25% of capital per trade.

25%?! Yes. And that is why nobody uses full Kelly. Because Kelly assumes you know your W and R perfectly, which is never the case. A 5% estimation error and you blow up. In practice, pros use Half-Kelly or Quarter-Kelly, which brings you right back to... 1-2% per trade. Full circle. The 1% rule is not an arbitrary convention, it is what the math says.

7. Circuit breakers — protect yourself from yourself

Prop firms (FTMO, Topstep, MFF) have strict rules because they know exactly how traders blow up. Copy them:

  • Max 2-3 losing trades per day → you shut down the computer.
  • Max 3-5% daily Drawdown → immediate stop.
  • Max 10% total Drawdown → one-week pause, back to backtesting.
  • Max 3 trades per day — beyond that, you are overtrading.
  • No trade within 30 minutes of a loss — revenge trading kills.

These rules are not negotiable with yourself. Write them on a sticky note on your screen. When you follow them, even a bad day stays manageable. When you break them once, you will break them again.

Over-risking = ruin

Risking 5% per trade? On a 10-loss streak (it happens), you lose 40% of your capital. You will need +67% to recover. You will not recover. The temptation of the "big score" is the first step toward an empty account.

8. Drawdown — understand and accept it

A 15-20% Drawdown is normal, even for a trader who is profitable over the long term. It is not a failure, it is a characteristic of the game. The best hedge funds on the planet experience 10-15% drawdowns and remain stars. What matters is not the absence of Drawdown (impossible), it is:

  • The depth of the Drawdown vs your long-term Expectancy.
  • The duration of the Drawdown (peak-to-trough then recovery).
  • Your ability to keep trading your plan during the Drawdown without increasing risk to "catch up."

An amateur trader sees a 10% Drawdown and panics. A professional trader sees a 10% Drawdown and keeps executing. That is the entire difference.

Going further

Use the position calculator before every trade and the journal to track your actual Expectancy vs your backtested Expectancy. Without these tools, you are flying blind.

9. Brutal summary

  • Risk 1% max per trade. 0.5% if you are a beginner.
  • Minimum 1:1.5 Risk/Reward. Ideal 1:2 or 1:3.
  • Calculate your position with the formula. Never "by feel."
  • Positive Expectancy or nothing.
  • Circuit breakers written down and followed.
  • A 15% Drawdown is normal. -50% is game over.

If you apply these six rules for one year, you will already be ahead of 95% of retail traders. Just that.
