MODULE 13

12-month action plan

The exact roadmap, month by month. No shortcuts. No miracles. Structured work.

⚠️ Absolute rule

You NEVER move to the next phase until the criteria of the previous one are met. If you skip a phase, you start over from scratch. The stats are clear: those who rush through phases lose their account within 6 months.

🗓️ Phase 1 — Foundations (Months 1-2)

Objective

Absorb all the theory, without trading a single cent. It's frustrating, and that's normal.

Weekly tasks

  • Week 1: Modules 1-3 (mindset, markets, chart reading). 1h/day.
  • Week 2: Modules 4-6 (price action, S/R, structure). 1h/day + 30 min live XAUUSD observation (without trading).
  • Week 3: Module 7 (full SMC). Redo it twice. Not easy on the first pass.
  • Week 4: Module 8 (Gold strategy). Write down the checklist. Learn it by heart.
  • Weeks 5-6: Modules 9-10 (money management, SL/TP). Do all calculations by hand. Use the calculator.
  • Weeks 7-8: Modules 11-12 (psychology, backtest) + Module 14 (brokers) + read Trading in the Zone (Mark Douglas).

Passing criteria

You've completed all 14 modules, all 14 quizzes with 80%+ correct answers, you've read Mark Douglas, and you can explain in 3 sentences: what is an FVG, how to calculate a position at 1% on XAUUSD, why recovery is asymmetric.

🔬 Phase 2 — Massive backtest (Months 3-5)

Objective

200 to 300 manual backtests of the Module 8 strategy on TradingView Replay. THIS is where you build your edge. Not by reading, not by watching YouTubers.

Daily routine

  • 1h of manual backtesting (5-10 simulated trades per day)
  • Each trade entered in the built-in backtester
  • Screenshot + setup notes + why you entered
  • Rating: plan followed (yes/no), emotion if it were real (imagined)

Passing criteria (MANDATORY)

  • ✅ Minimum 200 backtested trades
  • ✅ Expectancy > +0.2R
  • ✅ Profit Factor > 1.3
  • ✅ Simulated max drawdown < 20%
  • ✅ You know the market conditions where your strategy FAILS
If you don't pass the criteria

You DON'T have an edge. Period. You must adjust the strategy, redo 200 backtests. No question of moving to demo "to see". That's exactly how you've wasted 5 years.

🧪 Phase 3 — Demo account (Months 6-8)

Objective

Reproduce backtest stats in real time, with the psychological pressure of "fake money" (lesser but present).

Strict rules

  • Demo account of the same size as your future live account ($500-1000)
  • Max risk: 1% per trade
  • Max 2 trades/day, max 2 consecutive losses → done for the day
  • Mandatory journal for EVERY trade (via the built-in journal)
  • Mandatory pre-trade checklist (via the checklist)
  • Daily discipline tracker (via the tracker)

Passing criteria

  • ✅ Minimum 60 demo trades (~3 months at 5 trades/week)
  • ✅ Demo expectancy within 30% of backtest
  • ✅ Plan followed > 90% of trades
  • ✅ No day where you "cracked" (revenge, moving stops, overtrading)

💰 Phase 4 — Micro live account (Months 9-12)

Objective

Re-acclimate your psychology to real money. Real money changes EVERYTHING.

STRICT rules

  • Live account of $500 maximum with a regulated broker (see Module 14)
  • Risk 0.25% per trade for the first 50 real trades
  • After 50 trades IF positive expectancy → 0.5% risk
  • After 100 trades IF still positive → 1% risk
  • You NEVER reload the account if you lose it. You go back to demo.

Criteria for passing to year 2

  • ✅ Minimum 100 real trades
  • ✅ Positive net profit (even if just 5%)
  • ✅ Plan followed > 85%
  • ✅ No blow-up, no trade at > 2% risk

🛑 Signals that should make you STOP

Stop everything immediately if:
  • You skip the journal "because it's boring" → back to phase 1
  • You move a SL "just this once" → back to demo for 1 month
  • You take a trade off-checklist → 1-week break
  • You reload a blown account → full 1-month stop
  • You start looking for "a better strategy" after 20 trades → you haven't understood expectancy
  • You feel intense stress → you're risking too much, drop to 0.25%

🎯 The final truth

This plan is brutal because reality is brutal. Out of 100 people who read this module, maybe 5 will see it through. The rest will quit in phase 2 (too long, too boring) or blow up in phase 4 (not enough discipline).

But those who go all the way, statistically, have a real chance of becoming profitable. Not rich. Profitable. That's already huge.

Reminder

You don't trade to get rich. You trade to develop a rare skill that can generate supplemental income. If you go in with the idea of "recovering everything", you've already lost.
