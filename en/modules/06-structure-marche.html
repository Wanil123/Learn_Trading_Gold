MODULE 06

Market Structure

The skeleton that holds everything else together

Why structure is everything

If you had to strip everything off your charts except one single thing, keep market structure. Not indicators, not exotic candlestick patterns, not EMAs. Structure. Because structure is what price actually does — its highs, its lows, its direction. Everything else is just interpretation.

Understanding structure means always knowing: "the market is going up, going down, or going sideways — and to what extent." From there, you know whether to look for longs, shorts, or to stay flat. It is the foundation for everything that comes next (SMC, ICT, Order Blocks, Liquidity, etc.).

The 3 structural states

Uptrend: HH + HL

A bullish market makes Higher Highs (HH) and Higher Lows (HL). Each new peak is higher than the previous one, and each intermediate dip is also higher than the previous one. As long as this sequence continues, you are in an uptrend. Period.

Downtrend: LH + LL

A bearish market makes Lower Highs (LH) and Lower Lows (LL). The mirror image of an uptrend. Each peak is lower, each dip is lower.

Range

When you see neither clear HH/HL nor clear LH/LL — price oscillates between two boundaries — you are in a range. Range is the default state of the market. About 70-80% of the time. Most traders who lose do so because they try to "trade a trend" when they are actually in a range.

💡 Bias rule
In an uptrend, you only take longs. In a downtrend, only shorts. In a range, either trade the extremes (fade), or wait for the breakout. This discipline alone eliminates half your losses. No more "I feel it's going to drop" in the middle of a D1 uptrend.

Swing points: how to identify them

A swing high is a candle whose high is higher than the N candles to its left and the N candles to its right. Same logic for a swing low.

  • 3-candle pivot (fractal): N=1. Fast but noisy.
  • 5-candle pivot: N=2. Standard. Good balance between reactivity and reliability.
  • 7+ candle pivot: for identifying major swings, the "macro" of the market.

Practical tip: on your trading timeframe, use a 5-candle pivot for short-term swings and a 7-9 candle pivot to map the major structure that defines your bias.

BOS — Break of Structure

Break of Structure (BOS) is when price breaks the last swing high (in an uptrend) or the last swing low (in a downtrend). It is a continuation signal for the current trend.

Important criterion: a candle close above (or below), not just a wick poking through. This is an eternal debate in the SMC community — but statistically, the close filters out fake breakouts much better. Always prioritize the close.

CHoCH — Change of Character

Change of Character (CHoCH) is the opposite signal of BOS: it is when, in an uptrend, price breaks below the previous Higher Low for the first time. It is the first sign that a reversal may be developing.

Mental schema: HH → HL → HH → HL → (break of an HL = bearish CHoCH) → LH → LL (= confirmed bearish BOS)

A CHoCH alone is not enough to validate a reversal. But it is a first red flag telling you: "watch out, the trend is in danger, stop taking aggressive longs, wait for BOS confirmation."

Schema: HH-HL-HH-HL-CHoCH-LH-LL

Full sequence: three HH/HL in uptrend, then break of a prior HL (CHoCH), formation of an LH, then bearish BOS confirming the reversal.

Internal vs External structure

On an H1 chart, you have two layers of structure:

  • External structure: the major swings, the ones that also appear when you zoom out to H4 or D1. This is your "macro" structure.
  • Internal structure: the smaller swings between two major swings. This is the "micro" structure that exists within each leg of the macro move.

Internal structure is for timing your entry; external structure gives you your bias. You never trade against external structure without a very good reason.

Equal highs / equal lows: where Liquidity appears

When you see two swing highs (or more) at the same level, it is a sign that stops are accumulated just above. Traders placed their Stop Loss just above the last high. Everyone did it, and institutions know everyone did it.

Consequence: price will very often sweep those stops (liquidity grab / stop hunt) before reversing. This is the gateway to module 7 on Smart Money Concepts.

Multi-timeframe analysis: the order

The right method, the one that works:

  1. D1: define the macro bias (uptrend, downtrend, range). Non-negotiable. If D1 is ranging, you trade small or not at all.
  2. H4: confirm the structure and locate key zones (major swings, S/R).
  3. H1: look for setups aligned with H4 and D1. This is often where a pullback forms.
  4. M15: look for an intraday CHoCH in the direction of the bias, pinpointing the entry.
  5. M5 / M1: precise entry timing (optional).

Concrete example: D1 XAUUSD in a clear uptrend (HH/HL). H4 pulls back to a former resistance turned support. H1 consolidates. M15 prints a bullish CHoCH. You enter long, stop below the last M15 LL, TP at the last H4 HH. There you go. No indicator needed, just structure.

🏆 Structure is the foundation of SMC
Everything you will learn in module 7 (Smart Money Concepts, ICT, Order Blocks, Fair Value Gap, liquidity pools) relies 100% on structure. If you are not comfortable with HH/HL, BOS, CHoCH, SMC will feel incomprehensible. Take your time here — it will save you 3 months of confusion later.
⚠️ The "plastic" structure trap
Many traders adjust their swing definitions until the structure "fits" their directional bias. That is mental cheating. Fix your method (for example: 5-candle pivot, close required to validate a BOS) and apply it mechanically. If the structure says range, you do not take trend-following trades. Period.
